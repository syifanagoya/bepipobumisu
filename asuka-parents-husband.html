Who is Asuka? - Asuka Wiki

Asuka Wiki- Asuka is a Japanese professional wrestler. She was born on September 26, 1981, in Osaka, Japan. She has been signed to WWE, where she competes on the SmackDown brand as Asuka and is the current WWE Women's Champion in her third reign—she won the title as the Raw Women's Championship, which was renamed during her current reign. She began her professional wrestling career in 2004 with the AtoZ organisation, where she remained until her retirement in 2006. In 2007, she returned to the ring as a freelancer for organisations like as JWP Joshi Puroresu, NEO Japan Ladies Pro-Wrestling, Pro Wrestling Wave, Reina Joshi Puroresu, Smash, and Wrestling New Classic. She has won the JWP Openweight Championship, the Smash Diva Championship, and the Wave Tag Team Championship.

Asuka Career- Wrestling

Asuka Career- After becoming a fan of Keiji Mutoh, Satoru Sayama, Yoshiaki Fujiwara, Antonio Inoki, Akira Maeda, Nobuhiko Takada, Masakatsu Funaki, Volk Han, and Minoru Suzuki, Urai decided to pursue a new career in professional wrestling. Yuki Ishikawa is his trainer. Under the ring name Kana, Urai made her professional wrestling debut on June 16, 2004, for the all-female organization AtoZ, in a match against Leona. She spent the early portion of her career with AtoZ until abruptly resigning on March 19, 2006, owing to chronic nephritis Urai founded her own graphic design firm during her time away from the ring. Urai returned to professional wrestling on September 22, 2007, after a year and a half absence, and worked as a freelancer for promotions such as Ice Ribbon, JWP Joshi Puroresu, NEO Japan Ladies Pro-Wrestling, Pro Wrestling Wave, and Pro Wrestling Zero1, while also forming the Passion Red stable with Nanae Takahashi, Natsuki Taiyo, Ray, and Yumiko Hotta in NEO, which became her new home promotion.

Kana and Takahashi defeated Hiroyo Matsumoto and Kyoko Inoue for the NEO Tag Team Championship on October 10, 2009, Kana's first professional wrestling championship. They held the championship for two months until losing it on December 31, 2009 to Ayumi Kurihara and Yoshiko Tamura. Kana announced her departure from Passion Red on January 24, 2010.
