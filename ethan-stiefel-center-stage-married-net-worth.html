Ethan Stiefel Reunited With 'Center Stage' Co-stars

Publish date: 2024-04-21

Professional American dancer, Ethan Stiefel, reunited with his costars of Center Stage - Amanda Schull, Zoe Saldana, and Sascha Radetsky in September 2020. The main theme of their reunion is to discuss the impact that Center Stage has on their lives, even after 20 years.

Fans who may recall the movie Center Stage was one of the first movies that focused on the dance platform. The film helped launch the career of Zoe Saldana and Amanda Schull. At the reunion, they mentioned that 'it helped them realize that they truly wanted to devote.' The reconcilement of the casts of the movie also came with highlighted career impressions.

Ethan Stiefel, along with his costars, mentioned that they've heard from countless fans who felt empowered by their characters.

Ethan Stiefel's Married Life

Ethan Stiefel, age 47, is currently married to his wife named Gillian Murphy. Ethan Stiefel and his wife, Gillian Murphy was one of the ballet dream dancing couples after they shared the hand-holding moment in Center Stage movie back in 2000.

Ethan and Gillian actually dated for years and got engaged in 2011 as Stiefel proposed the love of his life onstage during American Ballet Theatre's Met season.

For four years, the lovebirds did not appear to initiate their plans for a wedding, but their nuptials came surprisingly. Stiefel and Murphy exchanged their wedding vows on 19 September 2015 in New York City.

Ethan Stiefel's Net Worth

Ethan Stiefel, who measures at the height of 5 feet and 7 inches tall, has an estimated net worth of $1.1 million, as per idolnetworth.com. He garners his fortune through his professional career of acting.

As an actor, Ethan Stiefel has appeared in several movies and tv shows, including Le Corsaire (1999), Center Stage (2000), Great Performances: Dance in America (2004), Center Stage: Turn It Up (2008), Center Stage: On Pointe (2016), and many others.

Besides, his acting credits include his roles in Great Performances: Dance in America (2003), Gossip Girl (2010), and No Days Off (2018).
