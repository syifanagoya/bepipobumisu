Who is Fred Warner?

Fred Warner is an American football linebacker who currently plays for the San Francisco 49ers in the NFL. He was born on November 19, 1996, in San Marcos, California, and stands 6 feet 3 inches tall, weighing 230 pounds. Warner attended Mission Hills High School in San Marcos and later played college football for Brigham Young University (BYU). In the 2018 NFL Draft, the San Francisco 49ers selected him in the third round, as the 70th overall pick.

Fred Warner has earned recognition for his outstanding performance, receiving First-team All-Pro honors in 2020 and 2022, along with Pro Bowl selections in those same years. His NFL career stats, as of Week 4, 2023, include 731 total tackles, 8.5 sacks, 9 forced fumbles, 6 fumble recoveries, 7 interceptions, 41 pass deflections, and 1 defensive touchdown.

Fred Warner's Early Life

Fred Warner was born in San Marcos, California, to Laura and Fred Warner. He's the eldest of two sons and one daughter. Fred comes from a diverse background, being half Mexican-American from his mother's side and half African-American and Panamanian from his father's side. Fred Warner Career

Fred Warner's career has been nothing short of remarkable. Starting at Brigham Young University (BYU), he made a significant impact in college, racking up tackles, interceptions, and accolades. His time at BYU showcased his skills as a linebacker. In the 2018 NFL Draft, the San Francisco 49ers picked Warner in the third round. In his rookie season, he started all 16 games, making an impressive 124 tackles and earning a reputation as a standout rookie linebacker.

The 2019 season saw Warner's continued rise to stardom. He changed his jersey number to 54 and showcased his talent with strong performances, including sacking Russell Wilson twice in a single game. The following years brought even more success. In 2020, Warner earned his first Pro Bowl selection and was named First-team All-Pro.

He signed a record-breaking five-year extension in 2021 and continued to dominate in 2022, earning Pro Bowl and First-team All-Pro honors again. As of 2023, Warner remains a force in the NFL, making key plays and contributing significantly to his team's success. His impressive career shows no signs of slowing down.
